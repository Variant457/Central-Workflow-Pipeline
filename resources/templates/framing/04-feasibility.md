# Feasibility Analysis

## Social Feasibility
<!--
Will the people involved support this project? Consider:
- Stakeholder Buy-In: do decision makers believe in this?
- Team Readiness: Does the team have the skills, or can they aquire them?
- User Adoption: Will the target users actually use what we build?
- Organizational fit: Does this align with how the organization works, or does it require cultural change?
- Change Management: What friction will adoption create, and how will it be addressed?

Be honest about resistance. A technically perfect project that nobody wants to use is a failure.
-->

## Technical Feasibility
<!--
Can we actually build this? Consider:
- Architecture: Does the current system support what we need, or are significant changes required?
- Technology: Are the tools, frameworks, and platforms mature enough?
- Integration: What external systems must we connect to, and are those interfaces stable and documented?
- Data: Do we have access to the data we need, in the format we need it?
- Unknowns: What technical questions can we not answer yet, and how will we answer them?

Flag anything that requires a proof of concept or spike before committing to delivery.
-->

## Financial Feasbility
<!--
Is this project worth the investment? Consider:
- Estimated Cost: Team size, duration, tooling, infrastructure, third-party services
- Expected Value: Revenue impact, cost savings, efficiency gains, risk reduction
- Comparison: What happens if we spend this budget elsewhere?
- Ongoing Costs: Maintenance, support, infrastructure after launch

Rough estimates are fine at this stage. The goal is to confirm the project is in the right ballpark, not produce a preise budget.
-->

## Schedule Feasibility
<!-- 
Can we deliver this in a reasonable timeframe? Consider:
- Target Timeline: Are there external deadlines driving this?
- Team Availability: Are the people we need available, or are they committed elsewhere?
- Dependenies: What must happen before we can start or finish?
- Risk To Schedule: What is most likely to cause delays?

If the timeline feels tight, identify what could be descoped to fit rather than assuming everything will go smoothly.
-->
